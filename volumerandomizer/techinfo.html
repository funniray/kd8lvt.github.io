I don't want the source code, I just want to know how it works!

Well, it's pretty simple, actually. Basically, I use Fast Simplex Noise, Random-JS, and Vol to:

  1. Get a random "Volume Biome" and volume level. (Fast Simplex Noise)
  2. I lower the values of the Volume Biome, because it tends to be (incredibly) loud. (NodeJS)
  3. Add them together, and then multiply by a completely random value (Random-JS)
  4. And then set your volume to the value, so long as it is between 20 and 70. (Vol)

In doing so, it creates a sort of "noise map". I just increment along the volume and biome grid by 0.001 every "tick", and once it finds a suitable value, it fires off the above process.

Once the first three steps are done, it slowly sets the volume, by waiting a quarter of a second between each volume increment.

After the whole process is finished, it makes a random number, waits that many seconds, then does the whole process again.
