What is it?


Volume Randomizer is a program for people with over-sensitive hearing,

making a way for people to train their brain to be less sensitive to auditory input,

without having to listen to classical music.

All you have to do is run Volume Randomizer in the background, and continue using your computer as normal.

Volume Randomizer does all the work for you, modifying your volume every few seconds.

All you have to do is listen to any music, watch videos, or even just play games!



How does it work?

Volume Randomizer does what it says on the tin; it randomizes your computer's volume, within a safe range.

If you want the technical details, or even look at the source code, go here:

Technical Info

In a nutshell, Volume Randomizer uses NodeJS and a few modules for it to:

  1. Get a somewhat random value, using Simplex Noise
  2. Modify it using a "Volume Biome"
  3. Make sure it's within a comfortable range for human hearing (between 20% and 70%)
  4. Set your volume gradually


Why make Volume Randomizer?

I made Volume Randomizer in the hopes of creating a safe, cheap, and fun way to help people like myself with sensitive hearing.

As a person who has suffered from incredibly sensitive hearing, and not being able to go to events with a lot of people because of it,

I decided to make Volume Randomizer, and then release it for others to use.



Is it safe?

Of course! Volume Randomizer is designed with the saftey of it's users in mind. It makes sure your volume is kept within a safe range.

And no need to worry about viruses or anything; I'm not that kind of person.

However, if your antivirus does think Volume Randomizer is a virus, it's because of the nature of volume control; I can't do much about it.
